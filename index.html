The Chess Pyramid

The journey from beginner to master in chess is one of the most challenging progressions in competitive games.

Only 1% of players ever reach the 2300+ rating threshold where FIDE titles like International Master and Grandmaster begin.

Each step up the rating ladder represents exponentially increasing difficulty, with small improvements requiring significant study and practice.

Journey to the West

Nikolay and Aaron are quintesential examples of the change in global chess.

Socioeconomic forces took these players from their home countries to come to Canada at a young age.

Russia and the former Soviet Union contained some of the most prolific chess players in the late 1900s.

India is an emerging power in the chess world, with numerous top young talents competiting on the world's stage.
